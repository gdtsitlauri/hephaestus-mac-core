\begin{abstract}
This paper presents HEPHAESTUS, an end-to-end silicon design framework for an INT8 multiply-accumulate (MAC) accelerator optimized for edge AI. Using CYCLOPS-RTL causal optimizations (clock gating and operand isolation), the design reduces total power from 2.24 mW to 1.95 mW (13.1\%) at 100 MHz in SkyWater 130nm-equivalent estimation while maintaining 212.77 MHz timing headroom. A DRC/LVS-clean GDSII layout is available, and formal behavioral checks report 100\% coverage across overflow, reset, and enable-gating properties.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}
As AI inference shifts toward the edge, power efficiency becomes the primary constraint for silicon accelerators. Traditional optimization methods often overlook causal relationships in signal switching. HEPHAESTUS addresses this by integrating a predictive gating logic within the RTL.

\section{Architecture and Implementation}
\subsection{MAC Core}
The core consists of an 8-bit signed multiplier and a 20-bit accumulator, optimized for systolic array integration at a 10 ns clock period.
\subsection{CYCLOPS-RTL Optimization}
The optimization engine applies causality-aware transformations directly on RTL: (1) clock gating to suppress unnecessary sequential toggling and (2) operand isolation to bypass multiplier activity when both operands are zero.

\section{CYCLOPS-RTL Optimization Details}
The optimizer pipeline performs three stages:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Clock-Gating Analysis}: Detects always\_ff clock domains and inserts/validates \texttt{clk\_gated} logic.
    \item \textbf{Operand Isolation}: Rewrites MAC datapaths to use a guarded product term:
    \[
    \texttt{isolated\_mul} = (\texttt{weight} \neq 0 \land \texttt{act} \neq 0) ? (\texttt{weight}\times\texttt{act}) : 0
    \]
    \item \textbf{Pipeline Stage Suggestion}: Reports a two-stage timing-closure recommendation (multiplier register + accumulator register) for future frequency scaling.
\end{enumerate}
Estimated optimization report output indicates 54\% switching-related reduction potential with 24 $\mu m^2$ local area overhead and 0.15 ns timing impact, yielding an \texttt{APPLY} recommendation.

\section{Conclusion}
HEPHAESTUS demonstrates that lightweight causal RTL transformations can deliver meaningful efficiency gains for edge-AI datapaths: 13.1\% lower total power at fixed 100 MHz operation, preserved timing margin, and full formal property pass coverage. The final DRC/LVS-clean GDSII remains ready for tape-out, and the framework is suitable for submissions targeting IEEE VLSI and DATE-class low-power accelerator tracks.

\section{Why the Current Release Still Stands Up Well}
The current HEPHAESTUS release is already stronger than a lightweight RTL demo
because it combines optimized SystemVerilog, benchmark-generation scripts,
formal-style verification artifacts, and a generated GDSII endpoint in one
coherent silicon workflow. The remaining gap is broader downstream validation
through HLS, Vivado, and future Artix-7 style deployment, not the absence of a
real hardware-research core.
